    What's a job series?

    The federal government categorizes jobs into groups or families of jobs or occupations. These groups are called a series. The series is a four-digit numbering system for organizing similar jobs. Related jobs will have series numbers that are closer together.

    For example, medical, dental and public health jobs will have a series between 0600â€“0699, where nurse jobs are in the 0610 series and nursing assistant is 0621.

    How did you find my match?

    The Job Exploration Tool uses a unique approach to finding job matches for you based on your interests. Unlike most job match assessment tools which match you based on skills, competencies, eligibilities, education level, or background, the JET aims to help you discover many different jobs which align purely with your interests.

    Why interest-based matching?

    Experts believe that interest is the cornerstone of a fulfilling career. When you are genuinely interested in your job, it not only leads to greater job satisfaction but also to long-term career success.
